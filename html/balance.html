Finding your School-Life Balance

As an undergraduate student, taking care of yourself while taking care of your studies can be a daunting task. Fortunately for you, we here at Serenebrum industries are ready to provide you with some of skills and tools you'll need to embark on your post-secondary adventure. Here are some of our best strategies you can use to find that perfect mixture of prioritization, organization, self-care, and self-awareness you'll need for an enjoyable, rewarding post-secondary experience.

Study Tips

Keep a calendar: Create your own calendar, physical or digital, where you can enter all of your schduled class times, due dates for asignments and exams, and schedule in blocks of study time. Set yourself up for success! By scheduling in every course milestone, you already have an idea of direction the course material is headed, and you can stay on track throughout the semester. By scheduling in study time, you can develop a routine so that studying happens naturally and you can hold yourself more accountable in getting your work done.

Don't forget to take breaks(but don't get carried away): taking 5 - 10 minute breaks during a study session is a great way to stay on task for longer periods of time without becoming mentally exhausted. Taking a short walk, stretching, even a short meditation can actually help you to be more focused when you return, just make sure that break time isn't suddenly stretching over 2 hours.

Stay hydrated: water is life. Make sure you're drinking lots of water throughout to maintain good health and stay clear headed.

Study with friends: join or even start a study group with classmates. Collaborating with other people is a great way to see things from new perspectives. Study groups can help keep each other accountable during group study sessions and remind each other of upcoming due dates to stay on track.

Write notes, in whatever form works best for you: Keeping digital notes is great if you have a laptop or some other device you can take to class, but writing out a second set of handwritten notes is an excellent way to reprocess information that may have been delivered quickly during a lecture and reorganizating the information in a way that makes better sense for you. Feel free to get creative! Add colour using highlighters, make a deck of cue cards, create diagrams; find the best way for you to digest the information.
